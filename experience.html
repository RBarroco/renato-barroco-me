My work experience in a descriptive way


Quantitative Research / Quantitative Trader

Stock and Futures Exchange, Sao Paulo - Brazil.

Navigating the experience of being an entrepreneur I learned the importance of decision making. If I could prove a decision with data much better. By working in the travel industry, I was exposed to the volatility of the dollar and was hooked by the idea of ​​becoming a trader, and an investor. I knew the importance of decision making based on data from my previous work experience at maistrip.com, and that is exactly what an investor have to do. In the past five years I collected as much data as I could and arrived at an investment thesis contextualized by the economic environment in which the world was. I had friends that I partnered with and participated in several discussions about these thesis. Financial and economic dashboards, business management and evolution of KPIs, balance sheets of central banks and companies, interest rates, forecasting of inflation, currencies analysis, correlation of assets for portfolio management, regressions, data analysis day in and day out.

  • Data Mining and Modeling: Collected, cleansed, and provided modeling and analyses of structured and unstructured data used for major short-term and long-term investment decisions. Outcomes:
    • A 75% reduction in the time spent for pre-opening market analysis, by automating report generation.
    • Improved correct price forecasting from 45% to almost 51% hit rate, increasing long-term gains.
    • Web scraping data from different sources that helped boost the quality of the model forecast.

  • Dashboards: Created visually impactful dashboards in Excel and Tableau for data reporting by using pivot tables and VLOOKUP. Extracted, interpreted, and analyzed data to identify key metrics and transform raw data into meaningful, actionable information.

Founder / Market Research & Finance / maistrip.com

Online Travel Agency, Sao Paulo - Brazil.


Maistrip.com was a dream come true. I always had the idea of  ​​building something from scratch, having the chance to put myself in an unpredictable environment. I see life as an opportunity to accumulate experience, at that time anything was more challenging than building a business myself. I had the great opportunity to develop a partnership with a friend of mine. His family had many years of experience in the retail tourism business, and I truly believe it was a smart move of mine to partner with them; having their experience with me, instead of going into the "jungle" alone. Branding, the positioning of our products, market analysis, field research against competitors, marketing campaigns, the financial management of a small company, the relationship with customers and suppliers, the experience of building our website from scratch managing developers, the analysis of data on a daily basis to understand the evolution of the business itself, it was all very exciting and very entrepreneurial. I see that experience as valuable as an MBA.

  • Applied quantitative data gathered to develop an understanding of customer behavior, product positioning, and lifecycle. Presented data that helped guide the company’s financial decisions.
  • Business Intelligence: Designed and built statistical analysis models on financial data sets that helped reduce costs (up to 20% per month), and opportunity costs (up to 25%) by managing cash flow needs.

Stock Loan Trader / Sales Trader Prime Brokerage

Morgan Stanley Prime Brokerage, Sao Paulo - Brazil.


At Morgan Stanley, I was privileged to work with a high level team. I was exposed to the North American work ethic, where I learned a lot. I had the opportunity to build a great relationship with the people I worked with, although the environment was quite challenging. The pace of work and the pressure to pay attention to details, especially since each transaction had a high notional value, made the experience incredible. I used to work with two other Stock Loan dealers, where I supported them and, at the same time, got involved with third parties to make sure everything was going the way it should. Prepare reports, manage stock portfolio, manage customer expectations by email, phone calls and face-to-face meetings, I used to receive over 60 calls a day. All of this in a super stressful environment where people sometimes were losing money. Being a calm and responsible person at that time was a fundamental behavior and that often made all the difference.

  • Researched market data trends and generated daily forecasting analysis reports.
  • Traded stock loan in a fast pace environment.
  • Interacted with international brokers prospecting new clients.
  • Coordinated financial transactions valued at more than $20 million.

Cadet / Infantry Division / Mountain Climber

Military Academy of Agulhas Negras, Rio de Janeiro - Brazil.


The Brazilian army was my first work experience. It was the first major challenge I faced in life. It started with the belief that one day I could get approved at a competitive process, that I would need to pass in a national exam. The exam includes a health test, a physical test and an intellectual test. As a cadet, you have a world-class educational system. From psychology, sociology, history, mathematics, physics, chemistry, computer science, strategy, command, military training, in my case mountain climbing and many different leadership experiences in the military field and outside the military field as well. The chance to manage others is a beautiful privilege. You end up learning more about yourself and you give back to them as well. If I had to define all my experience at that time, I would say the courage and the responsibility to set the example, it is a very heavy burden to carry, but it is a fundamental skill of leadership.

  • Applied strong leadership skills and problem-solving ability in a high-pressure environment.
  • Supervised and commanded a 33-person unit in mountain climbing special training.
  • Developed Cadet proficiency in planning, executing, and assessing complex operations, functioning as a member of staff, and providing performance feedback to subordinates.
